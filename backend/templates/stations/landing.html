Understanding the technology

What is an Automatic Weather Station?

Self-contained sensing system

An AWS is a mast-mounted instrument cluster that continuously measures atmospheric conditions temperature, humidity, wind, rainfall, and atmospheric pressure every few minutes, with no human operator needed.

Automated data transmission

An on-board data logger time-stamps every reading and transmits it to the WIMEA-ICT server via GSM or satellite at 10-minute intervals, delivering data from remote areas, lakeshores, and border areas.

Critical infrastructure for East Africa

Where observation networks are historically sparse, AWSs feed weather prediction models, power flood early warning systems, and give smallholder farmers the hyperlocal forecasts they need for planting and harvest decisions.

Real-world impact

Who relies on this data ?

AWS data from the system feeds decisions across sectors, from national forecasting to village-level agriculture.

National meteorological services

Ministry of Weather and Environment(Uganda), use AWS data as ground truth for their numerical weather prediction models and daily forecasts.

Ministry of Weather and Environment

Farmers & agro-advisors

Hyperlocal temperature, humidity, and rainfall readings help smallholders time planting, irrigation, and pest management , reducing crop losses across Uganda.

Agriculture

Flood early warning systems

Dense rain gauge networks like those deployed in Kampala enable real-time flood alerts,giving communities hours of warning before floods.

Early warning

Climate researchers

Long-term station records support climate trend analysis, hydrology studies, and Weather Research and Forecasting model validation, filling critical data gaps in East Africa's observation network.
